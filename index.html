Hi, I’m Jack (Daoheng) Niu

Quantum computing · Quantum Information · Deep Learning · LLM

I work on quantum error correction, quantum tensor network simulation, quantum algorithms, random quantum circuit and quantum supremacy. I have interests in a wide of areas in quantum computing and quantum information such as near-term applications of quantum computing, quantum communication, quantum complexity theory, quantum machine learning, etc.

Besides my quantum research, I have also huge interests in deep learning and LLM. I have finished several exciting side projects. More coming up in the Projects section.

Currently I am a 6-yr physics PhD at UT Austin. Previously I obtained my B.S. (high honor) with double major in physics and pure math from University of Michigan at Ann Arbor.

Projects

Tensor-Network Quantum Simulator

Python · JAX · cuTensorNet — 2025

Built a tensor-network pipeline for compressing and simulating NISQ circuits. Achieved exponential reductions in qubit/gate resources for specific classes of circuits; enabled larger instances of VQE and shadow-tomography workloads.

  • State compression (MPS/PEPS variants) with adaptive truncation
  • Differentiable contraction paths for hybrid training
  • CLI + notebook examples

QEC-Informed Quantum Repeater

Theory · Simulation — 2024

Evaluated generalized bicycle codes within repeater protocols; analyzed thresholds, resource overhead, and practical integration with photonic links.

  • Monte Carlo error models with erasures + depolarizing noise
  • Decoder benchmarks and distance scaling
  • Integration notes for hardware constraints

RL for Quantum Control

Control · RL — 2024

Trained agents to synthesize pulses for target unitaries on a dispersive cQED Hamiltonian, improving gate fidelity and leakage suppression under constraints.

  • GRAPE baselines + PPO/TD3 agents
  • Robustness to drift and T1/T2 variation
  • Open-source pulse library interface
